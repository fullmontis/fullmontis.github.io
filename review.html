No Country for Old Men - Cormac McCarthy (Book)

07/04/2017

A very powerful book. I found it very engaging. I already had seen the movie, and I think it did a perfect job to recapture the feeling of the book.

The book is harsh. Short, sharp phrases cutting to the point, story climaxes with a dry feeling. It is a book that really plays with what you expect of it, turning it up against yourself, and forcing you to consider reality in a new, and dark, way.

Anton Chigurh is one of the scariest characters to ever touch the page. It is the closest thing to a death bringer, a soulless and unstoppable killing machine. And yet he feels so real, it is impossible to not make some parallels with real life psychopaths. And by doing so, we are forced into thinking about how dark the human soul can be.
