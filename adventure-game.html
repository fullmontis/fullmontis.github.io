The Adventuring Game Rules

Introduction

This is a game for those who want to combine exploration of one's surroundings with a simple purpose to look up to. It is simple in the rules, but surprising challenging in the execution.

Step One: Finding your destination

Start by looking out of the window. It is preferable to do this during daylight to spot things better. Look in the distance for something that may look interesting. It doesn't matter what it is, nor how distant it is. Just pick the thing in the distance that you find interesting and that you want to visit for whatever reason.

Step Two: Find a way to reach the destination

The next step is understanding how to reach the spot you found. This is divided in two sub steps: finding the spot on the map, and finding a route to reach it.

To understand where the item of interest is located, there are a few tools available to the explorer:

GPS
This is probably the most immediate choice in the modern world. Everyone owns a cellphone that is GPS capable, thus making exploration a lot easier. It is definitely a viable tool to use.
Map
A map is the most important tool for the explorer. It allows to understand how various items in the scenery correlate visually and structurally, and shows roads and pathways that can be used to reach the new location. With Google maps, a huge database for basically the whole world is at disposal for everyone. Using a paper version is however viable and can be in fact more handy for a few different reasons, like taking notes.
Binoculars
Another indispensable tool, especially considering the nature of the game. It is excellent not only to pinpoint exactly what we are looking at is, which may not be especially easy for distant objects, and shows what is around it, making an easier task for triangulation.
Compass
No adventurer kit can be complete without a compass. This is indispensable to use the information we get on the map in the real world. It is also an excellent tool to pinpoint the location of the spot on the map.

Step Three: Reach the spot

The natural next step is going to the spot. Be sure to find out where the thing is, how to reach it and how distant it is. The more information you can get in step two, the easier this step is. This doesn't mean it is trivial, as in the journey itself brings a lot of logistic and practical problems that are ignored in the planning phase.

An incomplete list of the possible problems that may arise is shown here. Note that this is not complete and just acts as a reminder of what can rise up during a journey

  • Climate change
  • Road blocks and deviations from the planned path
  • Unequipped for the place the spot is (e.g. difficult terrain)

It is important to be quick on your feet so that you can get around these problems without many problems.

Step Four: Finding a new spot and starting the game anew

Once you reach that place, you need to do two things. First, spot where your house is relative to where you are. Second, start looking for another place in the distance that you can see from that point. Be sure to memorize it. This is your new destination. You can reach it right now, or just go home and reach it later. Once you resume the game, you just need to reach the new point you've spotted without going to the first place. However, it is useful to do so in order to understand better how to reach it.

If the place you spotted can't be reached because it is private property or the terrain doesn't allow you to do so, then you can just pick another interesting point near it.

Finishing the game

There isn't a definite moment where one has won the game. You can go on as far as you want, taking the last point and your home as a reference every time. Try to go as far as you can.

There are however some common ways to make the game more exciting by adding a purpose to the travelling:

  • Reaching as many spots as one can without losing sight of one's home
  • Seeing how far one can get in a specific time limit (e.g. a month)

This is of course rather flexible, so one can adapt the game to be as demanding or as relaxed as one wishes.

Create a journey log

It is suggested to take a picture of each new location for memorization and to create a log book of your journey. This is especially important later in the game, when you get distant from home and it becomes increasing difficult to locate the new destination.

Increasing Challenge: Tools and Movement

One may challenge oneself even more by not using electronic means, like Google Maps and GPS, limiting themselves to binoculars, maps and compass. Hardcore players may even go without using maps at all and just orienting themselves with the position of the sun or the stars and the scenery around them. It also makes for a more enjoyable and satisfying experience.

One can also limit the means by which one can move. One can ditch the car, or even not use any kind of transportation and just go on foot.
